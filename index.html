Central Texas Lawn Care Guide

Professional lawn care insights from 20+ years serving Waco area properties

🌱

Seasonal Lawn Calendar

Spring (March-May)

  • Early March: Begin weekly mowing as grass starts growing
  • Mid March: Apply pre-emergent for summer weeds
  • April: Fertilize with slow-release nitrogen fertilizer
  • May: Deep watering 2-3 times per week

Summer (June-August)

  • Weekly mowing: Cut high (3+ inches) to retain moisture
  • Watering: Deep, infrequent watering early morning
  • Pest control: Monitor for grubs and chinch bugs
  • Stress management: Avoid fertilizing during extreme heat

Fall (September-November)

  • September: Resume fertilization as temperatures cool
  • October: Overseed thin areas, aerate compacted soil
  • November: Continue mowing until growth stops
  • Cleanup: Remove leaves promptly to prevent suffocation

Winter (December-February)

  • Mowing: Bi-weekly or as needed for cool-season growth
  • Planning: Plan landscape changes for spring implementation
  • Equipment: Service mowers and irrigation systems
  • Preparation: Plan fertilization schedule for coming year
🏔️

Clay Soil Solutions for Waco

Understanding Waco's Clay Soil

Central Texas clay soil presents unique challenges but can support beautiful lawns with proper care:

Drainage Solutions

  • Aeration: Core aerate annually in fall to improve drainage
  • Organic matter: Add compost to improve soil structure
  • Avoid compaction: Stay off wet clay to prevent damage
  • French drains: Consider for chronically wet areas

Fertilization for Clay

  • Slow-release: Use slow-release fertilizers to prevent runoff
  • Phosphorus: Clay holds phosphorus well - test before adding
  • Timing: Apply when soil is slightly moist, not saturated
  • Iron supplement: Clay can bind iron - supplement as needed

Grass Types for Clay

  • Bermuda: Excellent heat tolerance, handles clay well
  • Buffalo grass: Native option, very drought tolerant
  • Zoysia: Slow establishment but very durable
  • St. Augustine: Shade tolerant, good for clay with amendments

Native Texas Plants & Landscaping

Why Choose Native Plants?

  • Water efficiency: Adapted to Central Texas rainfall patterns
  • Low maintenance: Require less fertilizer and pest control
  • Wildlife support: Provide food and habitat for local wildlife
  • Cost effective: Lower long-term maintenance costs

Best Native Plants for Waco Landscapes

Trees & Large Shrubs
  • Texas Red Oak: Beautiful fall color, good shade tree
  • Eastern Red Cedar: Evergreen, drought tolerant
  • Mexican Buckeye: Early spring blooms, small stature
  • Flameleaf Sumac: Spectacular fall color
Perennials & Groundcover
  • Texas Sage: Silver foliage, purple blooms after rain
  • Blackfoot Daisy: White flowers, excellent groundcover
  • Turk's Cap: Red blooms, attracts hummingbirds
  • Prairie Verbena: Purple clusters, spreads naturally
Grasses
  • Buffalo Grass: Low water, minimal mowing
  • Little Bluestem: Ornamental, fall color
  • Inland Sea Oats: Shade tolerant, interesting seed heads
  • Mexican Feathergrass: Delicate texture, drought tolerant
💧

Watering & Irrigation Tips

Efficient Watering for Central Texas

Proper watering is crucial for healthy lawns in our variable climate:

Timing & Frequency

  • Early morning: 5-9 AM for best absorption, less evaporation
  • Deep, infrequent: 2-3 times per week rather than daily
  • 1 inch per week: Including rainfall, adjust accordingly
  • Avoid evening: Can promote disease in humid conditions

Drought Management

  • Priority zones: Focus water on most visible areas
  • Mulching: Retain soil moisture around plants
  • Grass height: Cut higher during drought stress
  • Traffic reduction: Limit foot traffic on stressed grass

Irrigation System Maintenance

  • Spring startup: Check for winter damage, adjust zones
  • Regular inspection: Look for clogs, misaligned heads
  • Seasonal adjustment: Modify schedule based on weather
  • Fall winterization: Blow out lines before freezing weather
