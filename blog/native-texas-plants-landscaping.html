Native Texas Plants for Low-Maintenance Landscaping

Native Plants by Category

Native Trees for Central Texas

These trees provide shade, structure, and year-round interest with minimal maintenance:

Texas Red Oak (Quercus buckleyi)

Size: 30-50 feet tall

Features: Brilliant red fall color, drought tolerant

Best for: Shade tree, large properties

Care Requirements:
  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Deep watering first 2 years
  • Drought tolerant once established
  • Minimal pruning needed

Mexican Buckeye (Ungnadia speciosa)

Size: 12-20 feet tall

Features: Early spring flowers, interesting seed pods

Best for: Small yards, native plant gardens

Care Requirements:
  • Partial shade to full sun
  • Very drought tolerant
  • No fertilizer needed
  • Attractive to beneficial insects

Cedar Elm (Ulmus crassifolia)

Size: 40-70 feet tall

Features: Fast growth, excellent shade tree

Best for: Large shade areas, street trees

Care Requirements:
  • Full sun
  • Adapts to various soils
  • Very drought tolerant
  • Fast-growing native option

Native Shrubs & Small Trees

Perfect for foundation plantings, borders, and adding structure to your landscape:

Texas Sage (Leucophyllum species)

Size: 3-8 feet tall

Features: Silver foliage, purple flowers after rain

Best for: Foundation plantings, xeriscaping

Care Requirements:
  • Full sun required
  • Extremely drought tolerant
  • Avoid overwatering
  • Prune lightly in early spring

Pro Tip: Texas Sage is our #1 recommendation for low-water landscaping in Central Texas.

Flameleaf Sumac (Rhus lanceolata)

Size: 8-15 feet tall

Features: Brilliant orange-red fall color

Best for: Natural areas, slope stabilization

Care Requirements:
  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Very drought tolerant
  • Spreads by underground runners
  • Great for erosion control

Yaupon Holly (Ilex vomitoria)

Size: 8-25 feet tall

Features: Evergreen, red berries, dense growth

Best for: Screens, hedges, foundation plants

Care Requirements:
  • Sun to shade tolerant
  • Moderate water needs
  • Can be pruned to shape
  • Male and female plants needed for berries

Agarito (Mahonia trifoliolata)

Size: 3-6 feet tall

Features: Fragrant yellow flowers, red berries

Best for: Barrier plantings, wildlife gardens

Care Requirements:
  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Extremely drought tolerant
  • Thorny - good security plant
  • Attracts birds and butterflies

Native Perennial Flowers

Long-lived flowering plants that return year after year with minimal care:

Turk's Cap (Malvaviscus arboreus var. drummondii)

Size: 3-6 feet tall

Features: Red turban-shaped flowers, fall berries

Best for: Shade gardens, hummingbird gardens

Care Requirements:
  • Partial to full shade
  • Moderate water needs
  • Blooms summer through fall
  • Cut back in late winter

Esperanza (Tecoma stans)

Size: 3-8 feet tall

Features: Bright yellow trumpet flowers

Best for: Color gardens, hummingbird magnets

Care Requirements:
  • Full sun preferred
  • Drought tolerant once established
  • Blooms spring through frost
  • May freeze back but returns

Autumn Sage (Salvia greggii)

Size: 1-3 feet tall

Features: Continuous blooms, many colors available

Best for: Borders, mass plantings, containers

Care Requirements:
  • Full sun to light shade
  • Very drought tolerant
  • Deer resistant
  • Shear lightly in spring

Mexican Honeysuckle (Justicia spicigera)

Size: 2-4 feet tall

Features: Orange tubular flowers, velvety leaves

Best for: Shade gardens, hummingbird plants

Care Requirements:
  • Partial to full shade
  • Moderate water needs
  • Blooms late summer/fall
  • Dies back in winter

Native Ornamental Grasses

Add texture, movement, and low-maintenance beauty to your landscape:

Little Bluestem (Schizachyrium scoparium)

Size: 2-4 feet tall

Features: Blue-green summer, orange-red fall color

Best for: Natural areas, prairie gardens

Care Requirements:
  • Full sun
  • Extremely drought tolerant
  • Cut back in late winter
  • Beautiful seed heads in fall

Gulf Muhly (Muhlenbergia capillaris)

Size: 2-3 feet tall

Features: Pink-purple plumes in fall

Best for: Accent plantings, mass displays

Care Requirements:
  • Full sun preferred
  • Drought tolerant
  • Spectacular fall display
  • Cut back in late winter

Buffalo Grass (Poaceae dactyloides)

Size: 4-8 inches tall

Features: Native lawn grass, extremely drought tolerant

Best for: Low-water lawns, natural areas

Care Requirements:
  • Full sun required
  • Minimal water once established
  • Goes dormant in winter
  • Infrequent mowing needed

Designing with Native Plants

Start Small

Begin with a small area and expand as you gain experience. Native plant gardens evolve over time and look better with age.

Group by Water Needs

Plant drought-tolerant species together and those needing more water in separate areas for efficient irrigation.

Think in Layers

Combine trees, shrubs, perennials, and grasses to create a natural, layered look that mimics Texas ecosystems.

Plan for Succession

Choose plants that bloom at different times to ensure color throughout the growing season.

Establishing Your Native Plant Garden

Step 1: Site Preparation

  • Remove existing grass and weeds
  • Improve drainage if needed
  • Don't add rich soil amendments
  • Native plants prefer lean soils

Step 2: Plant Selection

  • Choose plants for your specific site conditions
  • Buy from reputable native plant nurseries
  • Plant in fall for best establishment
  • Space plants according to mature size

Step 3: First Year Care

  • Water regularly the first growing season
  • Mulch to conserve moisture and suppress weeds
  • Avoid fertilizing
  • Be patient - natives start slow but finish strong

Step 4: Long-term Maintenance

  • Reduce watering once established
  • Prune only as needed
  • Allow seed development for wildlife
  • Enjoy minimal maintenance requirements

Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Overwatering

Most native plant failures are due to too much water, not too little. Once established, most natives prefer dry conditions.

‚ùå Over-fertilizing

Native plants evolved in nutrient-poor soils. Rich fertilizer can cause weak, floppy growth and reduce flowering.

‚ùå Wrong Plant, Wrong Place

Shade plants in sun and sun plants in shade won't thrive. Match plant requirements to site conditions.

‚ùå Impatience

The saying "First year they sleep, second year they creep, third year they leap" applies perfectly to native plants.

Start Your Native Plant Journey

Native Texas plants offer the perfect solution for beautiful, low-maintenance landscaping in Central Texas. They're adapted to our climate, support local wildlife, and once established, require minimal care while providing years of enjoyment.

The key is starting with the right plants in the right locations and being patient during the establishment period. The reward is a landscape that gets better with age while requiring less water, fertilizer, and maintenance than traditional landscapes.
