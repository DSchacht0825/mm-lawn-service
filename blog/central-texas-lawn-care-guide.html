The Complete Central Texas Lawn Care Guide

Everything you need to know about maintaining beautiful lawns in Waco and surrounding areas

By M&M Lawn Service • Updated August 2025 • 15 min read

After 20+ years of maintaining lawns throughout Central Texas, we've learned what it takes to grow beautiful, healthy grass in our unique climate. This comprehensive guide shares everything we've learned about successful lawn care in Waco, Woodway, Hewitt, Robinson, Bellmead, and surrounding areas.

Understanding Central Texas Climate

Central Texas presents unique challenges for lawn care. Our region experiences:

  • Hot, dry summers with temperatures often exceeding 100°F
  • Mild winters that rarely see sustained freezing
  • Variable rainfall from drought conditions to heavy downpours
  • High humidity during summer months
  • Intense UV exposure year-round

These conditions mean your lawn needs to be resilient, drought-tolerant, and able to handle extreme temperature swings. The key is choosing the right grass type and providing proper care throughout the year.

Dealing with Central Texas Clay Soil

Most lawns in the Waco area sit on heavy clay soil, which presents both challenges and advantages:

Clay Soil Challenges:

  • Poor drainage leading to waterlogged conditions
  • Compaction that prevents root growth
  • Slow water penetration during dry periods
  • Difficult to work when wet or dry

Clay Soil Advantages:

  • Excellent nutrient retention
  • Good water storage capacity once established
  • Natural fertility when properly managed

Improving Clay Soil:

Professional Tip

Never work clay soil when it's wet! Wait until it crumbles in your hand rather than forming a ball. Working wet clay creates hardpan layers that can take years to correct.

  • Aeration: Core aerate twice yearly (spring and fall)
  • Organic Matter: Add compost annually to improve structure
  • Proper Drainage: Install drainage systems in problem areas
  • Avoid Compaction: Limit foot traffic during wet conditions

Best Grass Types for Central Texas

Bermuda Grass (Recommended)

Pros:

  • Extremely heat and drought tolerant
  • Handles heavy foot traffic
  • Self-repairing
  • Lower water requirements

Cons:

  • Goes dormant in winter
  • Can be invasive
  • Requires full sun
  • Needs regular mowing

Best for: Full sun areas, high-traffic lawns, drought-prone locations

St. Augustine Grass

Pros:

  • Shade tolerant
  • Dense, carpet-like appearance
  • Good heat tolerance
  • Stays green longer in winter

Cons:

  • Higher water requirements
  • Prone to chinch bugs
  • Can develop thatch easily
  • More expensive to establish

Best for: Shaded areas, areas with irrigation, homeowners wanting year-round color

Zoysia Grass

Pros:

  • Very drought tolerant once established
  • Dense growth prevents weeds
  • Low maintenance
  • Good wear tolerance

Cons:

  • Slow to establish
  • Goes dormant early and late
  • Expensive initial cost
  • Can be difficult to repair

Best for: Low-maintenance situations, established lawns, moderate traffic areas

Central Texas Seasonal Care Calendar

Spring (March - May)

Early Spring (March)

  • Apply pre-emergent herbicide before soil temp reaches 65°F
  • Begin mowing when grass starts active growth
  • Core aerate if needed
  • Overseed thin areas

Late Spring (April-May)

  • First fertilizer application
  • Establish regular mowing schedule
  • Begin regular watering if needed
  • Monitor for pests and diseases

Summer (June - August)

  • Mow frequently but never remove more than 1/3 of blade height
  • Water deeply but infrequently
  • Apply summer fertilizer (slow-release)
  • Treat for chinch bugs and grubs if present
  • Avoid heavy foot traffic during heat stress

Summer Survival Tip

Raise your mowing height during summer heat. Taller grass shades the soil and develops deeper roots, improving drought tolerance.

Fall (September - November)

Early Fall (September)

  • Continue regular watering and mowing
  • Apply fall fertilizer
  • Overseed cool-season grass if desired

Late Fall (October-November)

  • Core aerate if spring aeration was skipped
  • Apply winter pre-emergent
  • Gradually reduce mowing frequency
  • Clean up leaves promptly

Winter (December - February)

  • Minimal mowing (if any)
  • Avoid walking on frozen grass
  • Plan for next year's improvements
  • Service and maintain equipment
  • Monitor for winter weeds

Proper Watering for Central Texas Lawns

Watering correctly is crucial for lawn success in our climate:

Best Practices:

  • Water deeply and infrequently: 1 inch per week including rainfall
  • Best time: Early morning (4-8 AM) to reduce evaporation
  • Avoid evening watering: Promotes disease development
  • Use sprinkler test: Place cups to measure water distribution

Signs Your Lawn Needs Water:

  • Footprints remain visible after walking
  • Grass blades fold or curl
  • Color changes from green to blue-gray
  • Soil is dry 4-6 inches down

Clay Soil Watering Tip

Water clay soil in cycles: run sprinklers for 15 minutes, wait 30 minutes for absorption, then repeat. This prevents runoff and ensures deep penetration.

Fertilization Programs for Central Texas

Basic 4-Step Program:

Step 1: Early Spring (March)

Pre-emergent + Fertilizer

  • Prevents summer weeds
  • Provides early season nutrition
  • Apply before soil reaches 65°F

Step 2: Late Spring (May)

High-Nitrogen Fertilizer

  • Promotes active growth
  • Improves color and density
  • Include iron for clay soil

Step 3: Summer (July)

Slow-Release + Iron

  • Sustained nutrition during heat
  • Iron corrects chlorosis
  • Post-emergent weed control if needed

Step 4: Fall (September)

Winterizer Fertilizer

  • Builds root system
  • Improves winter hardiness
  • Pre-emergent for winter weeds

Common Lawn Problems in Central Texas

Brown Patch Disease

Symptoms: Circular brown patches, especially in St. Augustine grass

Causes: High humidity, overwatering, poor air circulation

Solutions:

  • Improve drainage and air circulation
  • Water early morning only
  • Apply fungicide if severe
  • Reduce nitrogen during humid periods

Chinch Bugs

Symptoms: Yellow patches that turn brown, especially along sidewalks

Detection: Part grass and look for small black bugs with white wings

Solutions:

  • Apply targeted insecticide
  • Maintain proper moisture levels
  • Avoid over-fertilizing with nitrogen
  • Choose resistant grass varieties

Grub Damage

Symptoms: Brown patches that feel spongy, grass pulls up easily

Detection: Find white C-shaped larvae in soil

Solutions:

  • Apply grub control in late summer
  • Maintain healthy, thick turf
  • Proper watering reduces attraction to egg-laying beetles
  • Beneficial nematodes for organic control

Iron Chlorosis

Symptoms: Yellow grass with green veins, especially in alkaline clay soil

Causes: High soil pH preventing iron uptake

Solutions:

  • Apply chelated iron supplements
  • Lower soil pH with sulfur
  • Improve drainage to reduce alkalinity
  • Regular iron applications during growing season

The Bottom Line: Consistency is Key

Successful lawn care in Central Texas comes down to consistency and understanding our unique climate. While our hot summers and clay soil present challenges, choosing the right grass type and following a regular maintenance schedule will give you the beautiful lawn you want.

Remember, every lawn is different. Factors like sun exposure, soil drainage, and traffic patterns all affect what your specific lawn needs. Don't be afraid to adjust these general guidelines based on what you observe in your own yard.
